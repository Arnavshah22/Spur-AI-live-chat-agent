# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Mobile shoppers on Acme Store, often mid-purchase on a phone. They open support when a question blocks checkout or erodes confidence: shipping, returns, product fit, or order status. They are distracted, time-pressed, and comparing this experience to every other chat widget they have tolerated.

## Product Purpose

AI-powered live customer support for a fictional e-commerce store. The chat resolves questions quickly enough that shoppers complete purchases instead of abandoning carts. Success looks like: a clear answer on the first useful reply, low typing friction, obvious paths to a human when needed, and enough trust in the agent that the conversation feels competent rather than performative.

## Brand Personality

Precise, premium, understated. The voice is calm and capable, not chatty or salesy. Craft reads in spacing, typography, and restraint; brilliance shows in texture and clarity, not flash. Support should feel like a knowledgeable shop associate, not a bot performing friendliness.

## Anti-references

Generic SaaS support widgets: Intercom-style purple accents, cream floating bubbles, stock illustration-heavy empty states, and the default "Hi there! How can I help?" tone. Avoid anything that signals template chat UI over a considered product surface.

## Design Principles

1. **Speed is trust on mobile.** Mid-purchase users measure support in seconds and taps. Every interaction should reduce steps, not add ceremony.
2. **Understated confidence.** Premium means legible hierarchy and deliberate materials, not decorative chrome. Gold and patina earn their place; they do not wallpaper the UI.
3. **Answer first.** Copy and layout prioritize resolution. No filler intros, no repeated headings, no marketing language in the thread.
4. **Mobile-native, not shrunk desktop.** Touch targets, thumb reach, and one-handed input patterns are defaults, not retrofits.
5. **Show competence in the thread.** Status, errors, and follow-ups should read as reliable system behavior, not afterthoughts.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Mobile-first pragmatism: 44px minimum touch targets, readable body type at arm's length, visible focus states, and reduced-motion respect for animations. Formal WCAG 2.1 AA audit is a stretch goal; contrast and tap usability are non-negotiable in daily use.
